# CHEN40140 Biopharmaceutical Engineering Project
## MEngSc in Biopharmaceutical Engineering — Summer Research Project
## Oral Assessment: Grading Rubric
### 5-Minute Recorded PowerPoint Presentation
### Experimental Projects Only

---

> **Scope:** This rubric applies exclusively to **experimental summer research projects** involving laboratory work. Examiners should assess content for the quality of experimental findings communicated, including evidence of practical problem-solving, honest reporting of negative results, and the ability to select and present the most significant findings within a strict time limit.

## Component A: Scientific Content (50 marks)

*This component assesses whether the student understands and can communicate their experimental research: the question, the approach, the findings, and their significance. The examiner should assess content independently of presentation quality.*

*Sub-components: Research question and context (10 marks); Methods (10 marks); Results (15 marks); Discussion and conclusions (15 marks).*

---

### A1. Research Question and Context (10 marks)

| Band | Mark Range | Descriptor |
|---|---|---|
| **A+ / A / A-** | 9–10 | The biological/engineering problem is established with confidence and precision. The significance of the research question is made immediately clear. The examiner knows exactly what the project set out to test or achieve and why it matters. Context is specific to the project, not generic. |
| **B+ / B / B-** | 7–8 | The research question is clearly communicated and the context is well-established. The significance is explained convincingly, though minor imprecision or excess background may be present. |
| **C+ / C / C-** | 6 | A research question is communicated but may lack precision or be stated only in general terms. Context may be too broad. The examiner can identify the project's purpose but may have to infer it. |
| **D+ / D / D-** | 4–5 | The research question is unclear or implicit. Context does not connect to the specific project. The examiner is uncertain about what the project set out to achieve. |
| **Fail (E+ and below)** | 0–3 | No discernible research question. Background is absent or disconnected. The purpose of the work cannot be understood. |

---

### A2. Methods (10 marks)

| Band | Mark Range | Descriptor |
|---|---|---|
| **A+ / A / A-** | 9–10 | The experimental approach is communicated clearly and at an appropriate level of detail for a 5-minute presentation. Key techniques, cell lines/organisms, and the logic of the experimental design are conveyed efficiently, ideally supported by a visual schematic or workflow diagram. The examiner understands *how* the research question was addressed. Any key optimisation is mentioned. |
| **B+ / B / B-** | 7–8 | The methods are communicated clearly. Key techniques and the general approach are identified. The level of detail is appropriate with minor omissions. A visual aid may be present but not optimally designed. |
| **C+ / C / C-** | 6 | The methods are described but the description is either too vague or too detailed for the time available. Key elements are identifiable but the experimental pipeline is not communicated as a coherent workflow. |
| **D+ / D / D-** | 4–5 | The methods are poorly communicated. Key techniques are not identified, or the description is so brief as to be meaningless. The examiner cannot determine how the experiments were conducted. |
| **Fail (E+ and below)** | 0–3 | Methods are absent, or the description is incoherent. No meaningful understanding of the experimental approach can be gained. |

---

### A3. Results (15 marks)

| Band | Mark Range | Descriptor |
|---|---|---|
| **A+ / A / A-** | 13–15 | Results are presented using clear, well-chosen visual outputs (graphs, images, chromatograms, etc.). The narration leads with the finding, not the method. Findings are selected judiciously — the student presents the most important results rather than attempting to cover everything. Quantitative findings are cited precisely. Each result is directly connected to the research question. If relevant, a negative result is briefly noted and its significance explained. The examiner leaves with a clear understanding of what was found. |
| **B+ / B / B-** | 11–12 | Key results are clearly communicated and supported by appropriate visual aids. The narration generally leads with the finding. Selection of results is mostly appropriate. Minor issues with precision or visual quality do not significantly detract. |
| **C+ / C / C-** | 8–10 | Results are presented and the main findings can be understood. Visual aids may be poorly designed, difficult to read, or not fully explained. The student may attempt to present too many results superficially. The examiner can identify the main findings but with some effort. |
| **D+ / D / D-** | 6–7 | Results are incomplete, unclear, or dominated by descriptions of what was done rather than what was found. Visual aids may be absent or illegible. The examiner cannot clearly identify the main findings. |
| **Fail (E+ and below)** | 0–5 | Results are absent or cannot be understood. Slides may contain raw data with no narration or context. |

---

### A4. Discussion and Conclusions (15 marks)

| Band | Mark Range | Descriptor |
|---|---|---|
| **A+ / A / A-** | 13–15 | The student interprets their findings confidently and accurately. They explain what the results mean in context and in relation to the broader field. At least one significant limitation is honestly identified. The take-home message is clear, memorable, and directly addresses the original research question. The examiner leaves with a strong understanding of what was found, what it means, and why it matters. |
| **B+ / B / B-** | 11–12 | Key findings are interpreted accurately and the significance is explained. The take-home message is clear. A limitation is noted. Overall convincing within the time constraints. |
| **C+ / C / C-** | 8–10 | Conclusions are present and broadly accurate. Interpretation may be superficial or may restate results rather than interpret them. Limitation may be generic or absent. The examiner understands the conclusions but the discussion is not fully convincing. |
| **D+ / D / D-** | 6–7 | The discussion is very brief, superficial, or missing. Conclusions may be vague or not linked to findings. No limitation identified. |
| **Fail (E+ and below)** | 0–5 | Discussion and conclusions are absent or incoherent. No ability to interpret findings is demonstrated. |

## Component B: Slide Design and Visual Communication (20 marks)

| Band | Mark Range | Descriptor |
|---|---|---|
| **A+ / A / A-** | 17–20 | Slides are professionally designed with a consistent, clean visual style. Each slide has a clear purpose and a meaningful headline title. Figures and visualisations are of high quality, clearly labelled, and directly relevant. Text is minimal. The visual design enhances communication. The overall aesthetic is polished and appropriate. |
| **B+ / B / B-** | 14–16 | Slides are well-designed and clearly support the presentation. Figures and labels are clear. Text may be slightly excessive in places. Good overall quality with minor inconsistencies. |
| **C+ / C / C-** | 11–13 | Slides are functional but design weaknesses are apparent. May contain too much text. Some figures difficult to read. Titles may be generic. Design does not actively hinder but does not enhance the presentation. |
| **D+ / D / D-** | 8–10 | Significant design weaknesses interfere with communication. Heavy text; little use of visual aids in results. Figures illegible or unlabelled. Inconsistent styling. |
| **Fail (E+ and below)** | 0–7 | Slides are of very poor quality or nearly absent in content. Results presented as text only or raw instrument output. Visual presentation actively hinders understanding. |

## Component C: Verbal Delivery (20 marks)

*Students are not assessed on accent, native-level fluency, or speaking style — they are assessed on whether their delivery effectively communicates the scientific content.*

*Sub-components: Clarity and pace (10 marks); Engagement and confidence (10 marks).*

---

### C1. Clarity and Pace (10 marks)

| Band | Mark Range | Descriptor |
|---|---|---|
| **A+ / A / A-** | 9–10 | Narration is clearly audible throughout. Pace allows the listener to follow without effort. Technical terms are pronounced clearly. The listener can follow the entire presentation without replaying. |
| **B+ / B / B-** | 7–8 | Narration is clearly audible and understandable. Pace is mostly appropriate with occasional rushing or slowing. Technical terms are generally clear. |
| **C+ / C / C-** | 6 | Narration is understandable for most of the presentation but clarity or pace issues are present. Listener may need to replay sections. *Note: Students speaking in English as an additional language will not be penalised for pronunciation patterns associated with their first language, provided content is understandable.* |
| **D+ / D / D-** | 4–5 | Significant clarity or pace issues throughout. Listener must frequently work to understand. Long inaudible passages, very rushed or very monotone delivery. |
| **Fail (E+ and below)** | 0–3 | Narration is substantially inaudible or incomprehensible throughout. Cannot be assessed from audio. |

---

### C2. Engagement and Confidence (10 marks)

| Band | Mark Range | Descriptor |
|---|---|---|
| **A+ / A / A-** | 9–10 | The student presents with evident confidence and conviction. Narration sounds natural and engaged. Tone and emphasis vary appropriately. The listener is left with the impression that the student genuinely understands and cares about their work. |
| **B+ / B / B-** | 7–8 | Reasonable confidence. Mostly natural with occasional scripted passages. Emphasis and variation present if not consistently applied. |
| **C+ / C / C-** | 6 | Narration is largely read from script in a flat tone. Content is communicated and the student demonstrates basic familiarity with material. Lacks energy but is not ineffective. |
| **D+ / D / D-** | 4–5 | Read verbatim in monotone throughout. Unclear whether the student understands the material. Engagement very low. |
| **Fail (E+ and below)** | 0–3 | No engagement with content. Student may be reading text they do not understand. Delivery cannot be assessed as a presentation. |

---

## Component D: Structure and Timing (10 marks)

| Band | Mark Range | Descriptor |
|---|---|---|
| **A+ / A / A-** | 9–10 | Clear, logical structure from introduction to conclusion. Each section transitions naturally. Timing within ±15 seconds of 5 minutes. Time distributed proportionally. Narrative is coherent as a whole. |
| **B+ / B / B-** | 7–8 | Structure is clear and logical. Transitions mostly smooth. Timing within ±30 seconds. One section slightly over- or under-weighted. |
| **C+ / C / C-** | 6 | Basic structure identifiable. Some transitions abrupt or missing. Timing within ±45 seconds, or one section noticeably disproportionate. |
| **D+ / D / D-** | 4–5 | Structure unclear or disorganised. Sections missing or in confusing order. Timing significantly off or time distribution severely disproportionate. |
| **Fail (E+ and below)** | 0–3 | No discernible structure. Cannot be followed as coherent narrative. Timing far from requirement. |

## Guidance Notes for Examiners

**Timing:** Record exact duration. Presentations outside ±15 seconds should have marks adjusted within Component D. Do not award A+ / A / A- in Component D for presentations outside ±15 seconds.

**Audio failure:** If embedded audio does not play, contact the module coordinator before marking. Do not award zero without first attempting to resolve the issue.

**Scientific content vs. delivery:** Components A and B/C/D assess largely independent skills. Do not allow excellent delivery to inflate scientific content scores, and do not allow weak delivery to deflate them.

**Experimental vs. bioinformatics content:** Experimental presentations may reference laboratory photographs, gel images, chromatograms, growth curves, and other lab-derived visuals. Assess whether these are clear, properly labelled, and effectively communicated.

**Language equity:** Component C descriptors explicitly accommodate second-language English speakers. Penalise communication failures (content cannot be understood), not accent or non-native grammatical constructions.

**Second examiner review:** All submissions awarded a grade of D/D- or below, or A/A+, should be reviewed by a second examiner before grades are released, in accordance with UCD assessment regulations.
